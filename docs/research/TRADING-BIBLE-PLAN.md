## How every indicator and signal maps to structure

When reading scans, TradingView, or options flow, interpret everything through this lens:

- Trend:
    - Uptrend: clear pattern of HH/HL on higher timeframe → only look for LONG setups that buy pullbacks to higher lows or retests of broken resistance turned support.
    - Downtrend: clear LL/LH pattern → only look for SHORT setups that sell rallies into lower highs or retests of broken support turned resistance.[^5][^6][^1]
- Structural events:
    - Change of character (CHOCH): break of the last major HL (in uptrend) or LH (in downtrend) → potential reversal zone; only consider reversal trades after clean retest and confirmation.
    - Trend continuation: new HH/LL with strong close and volume → trend is intact; join in direction of higher timeframe flow.[^6][^7][^2]
- Clean zones:
    - Identify supply/demand areas around prior structural pivots where big moves started.
    - Entry/target planning is always anchored around these zones, not single horizontal lines.[^6][^2]
- Volume:
    - High relative volume + structural break or defense of a clean zone = institutional intent, higher conviction.
    - Low volume + break = suspect, more likely fakeout or weak move.[^8][^4]
- Timeframes:
    - Higher timeframe (e.g., 4h/daily) sets trend and major zones.
    - Lower timeframe (e.g., 5–15 min) is used for precise entries that align with higher timeframe direction; ignore signals that fight the higher timeframe.[^9][^1]
- Scanner setups:
    - FRACTAL_MOMENTUM_BREAKOUT/BREAKDOWN: price breaking a recent swing high/low with high Fractal and Vol scores = structural break with volatility.[^3][^2]
    - ROLLOVER_MEAN_REVERSION/FADE: extreme swing low/high plus volatility cluster = structural extreme mean-reversion setup at the edge of an auction.
    - FRACTAL_OVERSOLD_BOUNCE / OVERBOUGHT_REJECTION: structural extreme + RSI extremity + vol = potential reversal at significant swing.[^2][^3]


## New unified daily checklist (your “bible” routine)

Use this every day; adapt times to your schedule.

1) Pre-market structure and scanner (around 7:00–9:00 AM)

- Open Elite Momentum Scanner (localhost).
- Verify:
    - GJR-GARCH = OFF.
    - Scanner parameters match your current Config sheet.[^3]
- Run full scans:
    - Record Market Regime, VIX, key index structure (HH/HL or LL/LH on QQQ, SPY, BTC, ETH).
    - Focus on symbols with Score ≥60, Fractal ≥80, Vol ≥50; respect Min Score ≥40 rule.
    - LONG list from LONG scan only; SHORT list from SHORT scan only.[^2][^3]
- For each candidate, write down:
    - Higher timeframe structure: uptrend, downtrend, or range (HH/HL or LL/LH).
    - Key swing points (recent major HL, HH, LL, LH).
    - Clean zones near current price (origin of strong moves or reactions).
    - Whether any structural CHOCH has appeared.[^6][^1]
- Update Google Sheet:
    - Live Signals: symbols, side (LONG/SHORT), Score, Fractal, Vol, structure notes, clean zones, VIX/regime notes.
    - Learning Flywheel: record today’s regime, VIX, and scanner conditions.[^3][^2]

2) Pre-open final prep (around 9:00–9:30 AM)

- Run Finviz Elite scans (LONG and SHORT URLs) to confirm liquidity and universe.
- Check Unusual Whales for options flow:
    - Note if call/put flow strongly supports or contradicts your structural bias; treat this as “bonus context”, not a reason to fight clear structure.[^3]
- On TradingView:
    - Load each candidate and mark:
        - Higher timeframe trend structure (HH/HL/LL/LH).
        - Clean zones and major swing points.
        - Volume behavior at those structures.
    - Confirm that planned trades will align with higher timeframe context, not fight it.[^10][^9]

3) Market open execution (from 9:30 AM onward)

- For each prepared candidate:
    - Wait for price to interact with your pre-marked structural level (clean zone, prior swing).
    - Only enter when:
        - The local lower timeframe structure turns in trend direction (e.g., HL forming in a higher timeframe uptrend at a support zone).
        - Volume behavior supports the move (not anemic break).[^11][^8]
- Define and place:
    - Entry: at confirmation of structural reaction/break.
    - Stop-loss: just beyond the structural invalidation level (below HL for longs, above LH for shorts).
    - Targets: next clean structural zone(s); plan to scale out 50% at 1R, 50% at 2R.[^2][^3]
- Apply risk rules:
    - Respect max 5% per position and 2% daily loss cap.
    - Use 3% trailing stop for open positions once they move in your favor, but never move stops away from structural invalidation.[^3]

4) Intraday management and additional scans

- Follow your daily scan schedule (10:00 AM, 11:00 AM, 1:00 PM) to:
    - Look for fresh structural breaks and new clean zones via the scanner.
    - Reject any signals that:
        - Fight higher timeframe structure.
        - Lack volume confirmation at key levels.
- For open trades:
    - Keep decisions strictly structural:
        - Hold as long as the structural stop level is intact.
        - Exit immediately if structure breaks, without debating “maybe it will come back”.[^12][^1]

5) End-of-day (around 4:00–5:00 PM)

- Record in Google Sheets:
    - Each trade: instrument, side, entry, stop, targets, outcome (R), and brief structural description of why you entered and exited.
    - Structural result: did the market confirm or invalidate your original thesis as seen in later structure and volume?.[^2][^3]
- Learning Flywheel update:
    - Update win rate, R:R, number of signals taken, and any pattern of structural behavior (e.g., CHOCH at major zones working better than continuation entries today).
    - Adjust scanner parameters only via your pre-defined parameter rules (e.g., if win rate <55%, adjust Fractal Weight, if too many/few signals, adjust thresholds).[^2]

6) Weekly review (Friday EOD)

- Review:
    - Weekly metrics: win rate, avg R:R, number of signals, parameter changes.
    - Journal notes on discipline: where did you break structural rules? Where did you respect them and get rewarded?.[^3][^2]
- Update:
    - Any refinements to how you define clean zones, structural breaks, and CHOCH vs continuation.
    - Risk rules if needed, but avoid frequent changes; focus on execution quality.
